Mad Honey

Mad honey is harvested once a year in spring when the Rhododendron flowers bloom in Nepal and Turkey. The honey is collected by scaling mountainside cliffs with only ropes and basic ladders for support, then using smoke torches to fend off the bees as they extract honeycombs. The harvesting is a team effort and requires extensive coordination among the hunters. The bravest ones will descend down the inaccessible cliffs that house huge, crescent-shaped hives.
